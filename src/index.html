You are an alien on planet XYZ, and you want to explore your planet’s moon, ABC. But how do you get there? To do that, we need to escape the clutches of planet XYZ’s gravitational field. But first, we need to understand gravity itself. In Newtonian mechanics, gravity can be described using Newton’s law of universal gravitation It states that $F = G\frac{m_1m_2}{r^2}$ is the magnitude of the force of attraction between two objects with mass, where $m_1$ and $m_2$ are the masses of the two objects and $r$ is the radius between them. $G$ is the gravitational constant, $6.67 \times 10^{-11} \ \ \text{N}\cdot\text{m}^2\cdot\text{kg}^{-2}$. You may notice that this equation does not take into account which objects the force of attraction is acting on, and that is because the force acts with equal magnitude on both objects, regardless of mass. The earth experiences the same force of attraction towards the sun as the sun towards the earth. Then, why does the earth orbit the sun if they are both being pulled on with the same force? That is because the sun is much more massive, so it is accelerated less by the same force of attraction.

Now, back to planet XYZ. Coincidentally, planet XYZ also has a mass of $5.97 \times 10^{24} \ \text{kg}$. To explore the vastness of space, you first have to start orbiting your planet, but what exactly is an orbit? An orbit is when you are travelling fast enough around a planet to not crash into the planet. You are always falling towards your planet, and gravity acts on you like normal, however, you are moving fast enough that you never actually crash into the planet. The speed required for such an orbit is given by $v = \sqrt{GM/R}$, where $v$ is the speed required for the orbit, $G$ is the universal gravitational constant, $M$ is the mass of the planet, and $R$ is the radius of the circular orbit.
